Liminal Photography

There's something fascinating about places that feel "wrong" or unsettling - empty spaces that seem to exist between reality and dreams.

What I Love to Photograph

  • Empty Parking Lots at Night - Those fluorescent lights casting eerie shadows, the complete stillness that feels somehow alive
  • Abandoned Buildings - Peeling wallpaper, broken windows, spaces where life once was but now only echoes remain
  • Fluorescent-Lit Hallways - Long corridors that seem to stretch forever, the humming of lights, that institutional feeling
  • Liminal Spaces - Places that feel like they're from a half-remembered dream... empty malls, stairwells, transition areas
  • Urban Decay - The beauty in deterioration, how nature slowly reclaims human spaces
  • Late Night Cityscapes - When the city sleeps and familiar places become strange and otherworldly

There's something about capturing these unsettling, transitional spaces that really speaks to me. They make you feel like you're seeing the world from a different angle.
